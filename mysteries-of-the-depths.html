The Wreck of the Edmund Fitzgerald


In her day, the largest laker on the Great Lakes was the Edmund Fitzgerald.

She was last sighted by the captain of a passing ship in the early morning of November 10th 1975.

Edmund Fitzgerald

The Edmund Fitzgerald set out from Duluth, Michigan, loaded with iron ore.

She was heading for the Soo Locks.

On Nov 9, the Edmund Fitzgerald sailed into a severe storm on Lake Superior that would batter her for hours that night.

What Happened To Le Griffon?

One of the oldest and greatest shipwreck mysteries of the Great Lakes is the disappearance of Le Griffon in 1679.

Le Griffon

Le Griffon was built on the shore of the Niagara River in 1679 by the famous explorer La Salle.

It was perhaps the first full sized sailing ship on the Great Lakes.

La Salle and his men sailed and hauled the vessel to Georgian Bay, and then to Mackinac Island and Green Bay where she was loaded with a cargo of fur.

The ship headed back to the Niagara River, loaded with valuable furs and La Salle stayed behind to explore further.

Le Griffon was never seen again.

Underwater explorers still search for the remains of this important ship from our past.

Disappearance Of the Bannockburn

This is the story of the Bannockburn, a cargo ship built in the United Kingdom and sailed on the Great Lakes.

Bannockburn

In November of 1902, the Bannockburn was headed with a load of grain from Fort William to Kingston Ontario when a great storm blew up in the Eastern end of Lake Superior.

She was spotted heading for the Soo Locks by the passenger steamer Huronic.

After that, she was never seen or heard from againaside.

A few weeks later, a lifejacket from the Bannockburn came ashore near the Grand Marias Lifesaving Station, the only debris from the Bannockburn ever found.

No one knows exactly where the Bannockburn lies, but there have been many reports over the last century of strange ghostly sightings of her, usually just before a storm is about to begin!
