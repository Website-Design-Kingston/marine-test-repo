ABOUT THIS WEBSITE

Off the shore of Kingston Ontario are some of Canada’s most intriguing and historically rich artefacts. Yet they remain out of reach to most Canadians, sunken beneath the waves of the Great Lakes. The Marine Museum of the Great Lakes wants to make those artefacts accessible through its new virtual exhibit: "Great Lakes Shipwrecks: An Archaeological Exploration".

This virtual exhibit enables visitors to be explorers, descend into the depths to discover a real shipwreck near Kingston, put archaeological clues together to piece together the story of a famous wreck, and to learn about undiscovered wrecks that remain to be found.

Created in partnership with active marine explorers, divers and archaeologists, the experiences presented are the work of those who have explored and worked to document and preserve these wrecks for decades.
